- **Date:** 2026-04-25
- **Decoding:** deterministic argmax under masked logits.

The first end-to-end Act 3 trace with `@narrate` + `@meta` + `@roll` +
mode-switched combat. Made possible by the recursive `gen.string`
grammar fix (commit `4da326a`); pre-fix this trace would have hung
indefinitely on the first narration.

## What's interesting

- **The `@narrate` / `@meta` distinction works.** The model emits
  `@narrate(...)` in-character ("Aria spots the hooded figure's
  hand…") and `@meta(...)` out-of-character ("Good perception from
  Aria; she's likely to notice something."). Same body shape (one
  string yield), distinguished by the call-prefix the runtime sees
  in the outer grammar. No XML tags, no post-hoc parsing.
- **The roll result drives the narration.** Aria rolled a 20
  (success). The follow-up `@narrate` describes her noticing the
  hilt — exactly what the high roll should produce. The model is
  reading its own KV.
- **Mode switch is clean.** `@enter_combat(hooded_figure)` flips
  the outer grammar; the next three samples are
  `@hooded_figure_attack`, `@aria_attack`, `@borin_attack`; then
  `@exit_combat(victory)` flips it back. No prompt manipulation,
  no API hops.
